### [1] [A Novel Framework for Transmitter Privacy in Integrated Sensing and Communication](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.16068v1)
*Vaibhav Kumar,Ahmad Bazzi,Christina Pöpper,Marwa Chafii*

Main category: eess.SP

TL;DR: 该论文研究了RIS辅助的多天线无线系统中的发射机隐私问题，提出了通过叠加信号和传输端人工噪声以及RIS的隐私感知传播环境设计来限制恶意传感器对发射机相关信息的未经授权推断。通过联合主动-被动波束成形设计，最大化恶意传感器的信道估计误差，同时满足通信质量、功率预算和RIS的约束条件。

Motivation: 随着ISAC系统的普及，共享无线电波形可能被恶意传感器利用以推断发射机相关信息，即使通信负载安全，隐私风险仍然存在。该论文旨在解决发射机隐私问题，防止未经授权的信道估计。

Method: 论文采用叠加信号和传输端人工噪声的方法，结合RIS的隐私感知传播环境设计。通过分析恶意传感器在非完美先验知识下的信道估计性能，提出联合主动-被动波束成形设计，利用交替优化框架解决非凸优化问题。

Result: 数值结果表明，RIS辅助的传播环境设计在保持可靠通信的同时，可以显著降低恶意传感器的信道估计性能。此外，隐私增益还能改善恶意传感器的到达角估计精度。

Conclusion: RIS辅助的设计在保护发射机隐私方面具有显著效果，能够在保证通信质量的同时，有效限制恶意传感器的未经授权行为。

Abstract: ISAC systems introduce new privacy risks because an unintended sensing node may exploit the shared radio waveform to infer transmitter-related information even when the communication payload remains secure. This paper investigates transmitter privacy, defined as limiting unauthorized inference of transmitter-related information through channel estimation, in a RIS-aided multi-antenna wireless system with a transmitter, a legitimate receiver, a malicious sensor, and a RIS. The malicious sensor is assumed to estimate the transmitter--sensor channel, and the resulting channel state information can then support unauthorized sensing, inference, or related signal processing. To mitigate this threat, we consider a privacy-oriented design in which the transmitter adopts superposition-based signaling with a message signal and transmit-side artificial noise, while the RIS shapes the propagation environment in a privacy-aware manner. The channel-estimation performance at the malicious sensor is first analyzed under imperfect prior knowledge, and both the true and predicted mean-square-error expressions are derived. Based on this analysis, we formulate a joint active--passive beamforming design problem that maximizes the malicious sensor's predicted channel-estimation error subject to a communication quality-of-service constraint, a transmit-power budget, and the unit-modulus constraints of the RIS. The resulting non-convex problem is handled through a numerically efficient alternating-optimization framework based on an augmented Lagrangian reformulation. Numerical results show that RIS-assisted propagation shaping can substantially degrade unauthorized channel estimation relative to the non-RIS case while preserving reliable communication, and further show that the privacy gains also improve a more direct sensing metric, namely the malicious sensor's angle-of-arrival estimation accuracy.
