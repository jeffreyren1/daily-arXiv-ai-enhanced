These results provide a photonic route to separate topological existence, skin-selected localization, optical visibility, and defective two-period dynamics in a non-Hermitian synthetic dimension.

### [2] [SNF-PRP: A Covert Integrating Sensing and Communications Framework](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.03960v2)

Main category: eess.SP

<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 集成传感与通信（ISAC）系统在同时进行传感和数据传输时，存在探测信号被拦截的风险，这不仅会泄露传输的信息，还会暴露传感行为本身。现有的物理层安全方法虽然能缓解信号拦截问题，但仍无法隐藏传感行为。为了解决这一问题，作者提出了SNF-PRP框架。

Method: SNF-PRP是一种基于伪随机噪声掩蔽的隐蔽传感框架，适用于OFDM基ISAC系统。该方法通过Kullback-Leibler（KL）散度建立ε-隐蔽性保证，利用Nsc倍扩展增益，并通过闭合形式推导出达到目标Cramér-Rao界（CRB）所需的最小积分长度。

Result: 在5G NR n78参数下的仿真结果表明，SNF-PRP能够在隐蔽性阈值以下（KL散度为5.8倍以下）实现亚0.5米的范围精度和亚0.5米/秒的速度精度，验证了其在-12 dB和-15 dB探测功率下的联合可行性。

Conclusion: SNF-PRP是一种有效的隐蔽传感框架，能够在低探测功率下实现高精度传感，同时保证隐蔽性。这一成果为ISAC系统的安全性提供了新的解决方案。

Abstract: Integrated sensing and communication (ISAC) enables simultaneous sensing and data transmission but exposes a critical vulnerability: probing signals may be intercepted, revealing both the transmitted information and the act of sensing itself. Existing physical layer security approaches mitigate interception yet operate with detectable signals, leaving sensing activity observable to a passive warden. This paper introduces sub-noise-floor pseudo-random probing (SNF-PRP), a covert sensing framework for OFDM-based ISAC systems under an energy-detection adversary model. SNF-PRP establishes an $ε$-covertness guarantee via Kullback-Leibler (KL) divergence, exploits an $N_{\mathrm{sc}}$-fold spreading gain absent from prior wideband analyses, and derives in closed form the minimum integration length required to achieve a target Cramér-Rao bound (CRB). Simulations under 5G~NR n78 numerology confirm sub-0.5\,m range and sub-0.5\,m/s velocity accuracy with KL divergence $5.8\times$ below the covertness threshold, validating joint feasibility at $-12$\,dB and $-15$\,dB probing powers.

### [3] [Rate-Splitting--Inspired Uplink Near-Field ISAC](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.07091v1)
*Anup Mishra,Israel Leyva-Mayorga,Petar Popovski*

Main category: eess.SP

TL;DR: 该论文提出了一种基于速率分割（RS）的上行链路近场集成感知与通信（ISAC）框架，旨在解决感知与通信功能共享资源时的干扰问题，并研究其性能边界和大阵列极限。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 集成感知与通信（ISAC）需要在共享频谱、硬件和信号处理资源的同时，解决感知与通信功能间的干扰问题，尤其是在上行链路操作中。传统的非正交多址（NOMA）方案存在局限性，本文提出一种RS启发的框架以优化性能边界。

Method: 提出了RS启发的上行链路近场ISAC框架，将通信消息分割到感知操作中，推导了通信速率（CR）和感知速率（SR）的闭式表达式，考虑了目标响应估计不确定性引起的残余干扰。通过大阵列极限分析验证了模型的物理一致性。

Result: 研究发现，RS框架可以包含NOMA的时间共享区域，并在某些情况下严格扩大感知与通信的权衡边界。高信噪比分析表明，残余干扰会影响速率偏移但不影响主导斜率，而在完全对齐的情况下会限制斜率。数值结果验证了RS方案的优势和残余干扰的影响。

Conclusion: 本文的RS启发框架在解决上行链路近场ISAC中的感知与通信干扰问题上表现出色，通过速率分割和非正交多址的结合优化了性能边界，同时考虑了物理一致性的近场模型，为大阵列设计提供了理论支持。

Abstract: Integrated sensing and communication (ISAC) enables sensing and communication (S&C) functionalities to share spectrum, hardware, and signal-processing resources, but the resulting inter-functionality interference creates a fundamental receiver-design challenge, particularly in uplink operation. This paper develops a rate-splitting (RS)-inspired framework for uplink near-field ISAC. The framework generalizes the sensing-centric (S-C) and communication-centric (C-C) endpoint orders of non-orthogonal multiple access (NOMA)-inspired ISAC by splitting the communication message across the sensing operation. Closed-form expressions are derived for the communication-rate (CR) and sensing-rate (SR), accounting for residual sensing interference from target-response estimation uncertainty. The achievable CR-SR rate region is characterized under sensing-matched illumination, where the proposed single-frame RS-inspired boundary contains the NOMA-inspired time-sharing region. Unlike the classical Gaussian uplink multiple access channel, where RS recovers the time-sharing dominant face, the split factor in uplink ISAC also reshapes the sensing-stage interference, allowing the RS-inspired boundary to match or strictly enlarge the S&C tradeoff. High-SNR analysis shows that, for non-aligned S&C channels, residual sensing interference changes the rate offsets but not the leading S&C slopes, whereas in the fully-aligned case it becomes slope-limiting. Using an aperture-aware near-field channel model, large-array limits are derived, showing that achievable rates remain finite as the array grows. Numerical results validate the analysis and demonstrate the benefits of the RS-inspired scheme, the impact of residual sensing interference, and the bounded large-array behaviour induced by physically consistent near-field modelling.
