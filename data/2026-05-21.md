Abstract: We investigate controlling the lateral Goos-Hänchen (GH) shift for a TM-polarized field reflected from Otto structure containing four level N-type atomic medium. The N-type atomic configuration can be formed by coupling the standard three-level $Λ$ system to an additional upper energy level through a coherent driving field. The medium can then be switched from transparent to absorptive under the effect of the driving field. In the Otto structure, an air gap typically separates a dielectric prism from a metal film. We show that the sign and magnitude of the GH shift can be highly controlled when the air gap is replaced by the coherent atomic medium. This can be achieved by adjusting the strength of the applied fields to the atomic medium, while the geometrical characteristics of the proposed structure are unchanged.

### [2] [Scalable native signed optical computing enabled by dual-wavelength incoherent multiplexing](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.20864v1)

Motivation: 非相干光子神经网络（PNNs）虽然在模拟光学计算中具有鲁棒性，但实现原生有符号操作仍然存在挑战。现有方法通常需要额外的空间通道或时间编码来表示双极性输入信号，导致硬件开销随着系统规模增加。本文旨在解决这一问题。

Method: 作者提出了一种基于双波长的非相干光子架构，该架构将互补信号分量编码到两个波长通道上，并在共享物理路径中执行计算。这种方法消除了重复的加权单元，从而使与有符号计算相关的额外硬件开销保持恒定。

Result: 实验制备的器件表现出超过40 GHz的调制带宽，并实现了四象限光学乘法，标准差误差为1.27%。在系统级功能验证中，该架构在Moons数据集上达到95.1%的分类准确率，在MNIST数据集上达到91.63%。

Conclusion: 本文的创新点在于提出了一种高效的双波长非相干光子架构，实现了原生有符号计算，且硬件开销与矩阵大小无关。该方法为可扩展的非相干光子计算系统提供了实用路径。

Abstract: Incoherent photonic neural networks (PNNs) provide a robust platform for analog optical computing, yet efficient implementation of native signed operations remains challenging. Existing incoherent PNNs approaches often require additional spatial channels or temporal encoding steps to represent bipolar input signals, resulting in hardware overhead that scales with system size. Here, we demonstrate a dual-wavelength incoherent photonic architecture that natively supports both signed inputs and signed weights on a thin-film lithium niobate platform. By encoding complementary signal components onto two wavelength channels and performing computation within a shared physical path, the proposed scheme eliminates duplicated weighting units. As a result, the additional hardware overhead associated with signed computation remains constant per multiply accumulate operation, independent of matrix size. The fabricated device exhibits a modulation bandwidth exceeding 40 GHz and achieves four-quadrant optical multiplication with a standard deviation error of 1.27%. System-level functionality is validated through neural-network classification, achieving 95.1% accuracy on the Moons dataset and 91.63% on MNIST. These results establish a practical route toward scalable incoherent photonic computing systems with native bipolar processing capability.
