### [1] [Zero Knowledge Verification of Transaction Guides for P2P Energy Trading in Distribution Networks](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12085v1)
*Hyunjoong Kim*

Main category: eess.SY

TL;DR: 本文通过零知识证明方法解决了P2P能源交易中网络敏感数据保密性与交易指南可验证性之间的矛盾，提出了一种基于计算完整性验证的网络约束交易指南方案。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 在P2P能源交易中，基于网络敏感性的协调机制会导致一个关键问题：敏感数据的披露可能泄露网络脆弱性，而不披露又无法让交易参与者验证交易指南的正确性。本文旨在解决这一矛盾。

Method: 作者提出了一种基于零知识证明的方法，通过将平衡、电压、线路流量和最优性等条件编码为算术电路（R1CS约束和二次算术程序），利用双线性配对验证计算完整性。同时，区块链技术用于绑定已批准的电路、公共输入、声明标识、证明和验证结果，确保审计的可追溯性。

Result: 在改进的IEEE 33总线系统案例研究中，该方法展示了交易指南在满足网络约束、抵御公共输入和见证不一致攻击的能力，且证明大小仅为806字节，具备实际应用可行性。

Conclusion: 该方法不仅解决了网络敏感数据保密性与交易指南可验证性之间的冲突，还通过区块链技术增强了透明度和抗攻击能力，为P2P能源交易的安全性提供了新的技术路径。

Abstract: Peer-to-peer (P2P) energy trading requires network-aware coordination because transactions are physically realized through distribution networks. However, sensitivity-based coordination causes a confidentiality-verifiability tradeoff, as network sensitivities may reveal vulnerable components while undisclosed sensitivities prevent participants from verifying utility-provided transaction guides. This paper proposes a zero-knowledge-proof-based method for verifying the computational integrity of network-constrained transaction guides with respect to committed private network data, without exposing network-sensitivity information. The guide defines admissible injection and withdrawal volumes derived from sign-decomposed sensitivity matrices while satisfying balance, voltage, line-flow, and optimality conditions. These conditions are encoded in an arithmetic circuit, represented as R1CS constraints and a quadratic arithmetic program, and verified using a bilinear pairing. Blockchain commitments bind the approved circuit, public inputs, statement identifiers, proof, and verification result for tamper-evident auditability. The proposed proof certifies correct guide computation from committed network data; the authenticity of the committed network data is handled through an explicit registration and attestation assumption. Case studies on a modified IEEE 33-bus system show satisfaction of network constraints after clearing, rejection of public-input and witness-inconsistency attacks, and practical on-chain overhead, with an 806-byte proof.

### [2] [Additive Noise, Shift Recovery, and Signed Signals in the Cumulative Distribution Transform](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11432v1)
*Harbir Antil,Ratna Khatri,Aryan Saxena*

Main category: cs.IT

TL;DR: 该论文研究了累积分布变换（CDT）在一维平移和加性扰动下的行为，并利用其结构进行平移恢复。通过局部非退化条件，推导了一阶展开式，并分析了噪声在CDT空间的影响。论文还提出了在已知和未知模板情况下恢复平移的方法，并通过数值实验验证了其有效性。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 研究累积分布变换（CDT）在加性扰动下的行为及其在平移恢复中的应用，以解决信号处理中基于密度的平移恢复问题。

Method: 论文采用了一阶展开式来量化加性噪声对CDT的影响，并在已知和未知模板的设定下提出了平移恢复的方法，包括投影法和交替对齐与平均法。此外，还提出了基于有符号累积分布变换（SCDT）的方法来扩展到有符号信号的恢复。

Result: 通过理论推导和数值实验，论文展示了在CDT空间中噪声扰动的影响以及恢复方法的稳定性和有效性。特别是在低密度区域，扰动会被放大，而高斯随机场的加性噪声在CDT空间中仍然保持高斯性。恢复方法在无噪声情况下表现出精确性，在噪声情况下也具有良好的稳定性。

Conclusion: 论文证明了CDT能够有效地用于信号恢复中的平移估计，尤其是在密度和有符号信号的处理中表现出优越性能。通过理论分析和实验验证，论文为基于CDT的平移恢复提供了系统性的方法。

Abstract: The cumulative distribution transform (CDT) is a quantile-based transport representation that exactly linearizes one-dimensional translations of positive densities. We study how this structure behaves under additive perturbations and how it can be exploited for shift recovery. Under a local nondegeneracy condition, we derive a first-order expansion showing that additive noise in physical space induces a nonlocal perturbation in CDT space through the primitive of the noise, weighted by the reciprocal density. This yields an explicit description of transform-domain sensitivity and shows, in particular, that perturbations are amplified in low-density regions. When the physical-space perturbation is modeled as a centered Gaussian random field, the induced first-order CDT perturbation is again Gaussian, with an explicit covariance kernel.   We then use this structure to study recovery in CDT coordinates. In the known-template setting, the transport shift is obtained by projection onto the constant mode, giving an explicit estimator together with exactness in the noiseless case and a stability bound under perturbations. In the unknown-template setting, multiple observations permit joint recovery of the shifts and a common template up to the natural constant-mode gauge, leading to a simple de-shift--and--average procedure. We also consider a signed-signal analogue based on the signed cumulative distribution transform (SCDT), where shifts are estimated numerically by feature matching and unknown templates are recovered by alternating alignment and averaging. Numerical experiments validate the perturbation analysis and illustrate effective recovery for both density-valued and signed signals.
