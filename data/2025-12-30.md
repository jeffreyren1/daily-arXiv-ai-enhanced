Method: 论文采用了全微磁模拟和Thiele集体坐标模型的结合方法。首先通过数值模拟观察了斯格明子对的螺旋轨迹和振荡行为，然后通过解析模型推导了径向漂移、角衰减和分离距离的对数增长的表达式，并结合微磁连续分析解释了长程排斥作用的起源。

Result: 研究发现，斯格明子对的相互作用表现为长程指数排斥势，其螺旋运动模式和振荡行为与Gilbert阻尼密切相关。解析模型与数值结果高度一致，证实了相互作用势的指数形式。此外，斯格明子尾部的微磁连续分析解释了长程排斥的物理机制。

Conclusion: 论文建立了一个统一的理论框架，可以解释斯格明子对的螺旋运动、呼吸动态和长程排斥作用。这些发现为多斯格明子信息载体和集体器件的设计提供了重要指导。

Abstract: Magnetic skyrmions exhibit particle-like stability and rich dynamical behaviour arising from their topological nature, making them promising building blocks for future spintronic devices. In this work, we investigate the interaction dynamics of two Neel-type skyrmions in an ultrathin ferromagnetic film through a combined analytical and numerical approach. Full micromagnetic simulations reveal that when the initial separation exceeds twice the skyrmion radius, the pair undergoes a repulsive interaction leading to an outward spiral trajectory whose radial and angular components depend sensitively on the Gilbert damping, consistent with particle-like models of skyrmion motion. The simulations further show a two-component oscillatory behaviour in the out-of-plane magnetization: a fast intrinsic breathing mode superimposed on a slower interaction-induced modulation. Using Thiele collective-coordinate model, an expression for the radial drift, angular decay, and logarithmic growth of the separation distance is derived. The analytical predictions show excellent agreement with numerical results, confirming the exponential form of the long-range interaction potential. A continuum micromagnetic analysis of the skyrmion tail explains the origin of the exponential decay and its role in governing the interaction strength. Together, these findings provide a unified framework for understanding spiral motion, breathing dynamics, and long-range repulsion in interacting skyrmion systems, offering insights relevant for multi-skyrmion information carriers and collective skyrmion-based devices.
