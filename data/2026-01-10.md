### [1] [End-to-end differentiable design of geometric waveguide displays](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.04370)
*Xinge Yang,Zhaocheng Liu,Zhaoyu Nie,Qingyuan Fan,Zhimin Shi,Jim Bonar,Wolfgang Heidrich*

Main category: physics.optics

TL;DR: 该论文提出了一个端到端的可微分优化框架，用于联合优化几何波导中的非顺序光传输和偏振依赖性多层薄膜涂层，从而显著提高光效率、eyebox和视场均匀性。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 几何波导在光学透视增强现实显示中具有潜力，但其性能受限于非顺序光传输和偏振依赖性多层薄膜涂层的联合优化难题。作者旨在解决这一问题，通过可微分优化方法提升系统性能。

Method: 作者结合了非顺序蒙特卡罗偏振光线追踪和可微分传递矩阵薄膜求解器，提出了一种端到端的可微分优化框架。该方法通过内存节省策略，支持在单台多GPU工作站上优化上千个层厚度参数和数十亿的非顺序光线-表面交点。此外，通过从过度参数化的堆栈开始并在离散可制造性约束下驱动冗余层厚度为零，实现了自动层修剪和拓扑优化。

Result: 代表性设计中，随机初始化后，光效率从4.1%提升至33.5%，eyebox和视场均匀性分别提高了约17倍和11倍。此外，作者还联合优化了波导和图像预处理网络，进一步提升了感知图像质量。

Conclusion: 该框架不仅实现了波导内系统级、高维度涂层优化，还为下一代光学设计的可微分光学提供了新的可能性。

Abstract: Geometric waveguides are a promising architecture for optical see-through augmented reality displays, but their performance is severely bottlenecked by the difficulty of jointly optimizing non-sequential light transport and polarization-dependent multilayer thin-film coatings. Here we present the first end-to-end differentiable optimization framework for geometric waveguide that couples non-sequential Monte Carlo polarization ray tracing with a differentiable transfer-matrix thin-film solver. A differentiable Monte Carlo ray tracer avoids the exponential growth of deterministic ray splitting while enabling gradients backpropagation from eyebox metrics to design parameters. With memory-saving strategies, we optimize more than one thousand layer-thickness parameters and billions of non-sequential ray-surface intersections on a single multi-GPU workstation. Automated layer pruning is achieved by starting from over-parameterized stacks and driving redundant layers to zero thickness under discrete manufacturability constraints, effectively performing topology optimization to discover optimal coating structures. On a representative design, starting from random initialization within thickness bounds, our method increases light efficiency from 4.1\% to 33.5\% and improves eyebox and FoV uniformity by $\sim$17$\times$ and $\sim$11$\times$, respectively. Furthermore, we jointly optimize the waveguide and an image preprocessing network to improve perceived image quality. Our framework not only enables system-level, high-dimensional coating optimization inside the waveguide, but also expands the scope of differentiable optics for next-generation optical design.

### [2] [Processing-Dependent Near-Field Radiative Heat Transfer at Au/SiC Interfaces](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.04452)
*A. Márquez,R. Esquivel-Sirvent*

Main category: physics.optics

TL;DR: 本文研究了热退火处理对金薄膜/碳化硅基板上纳米尺度真空间隙近场辐射传热的影响，发现退火可显著改变低频介电损耗，增强表面等离子体模式的耦合，从而提升近场热传递效率。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 热退火是一种广泛应用的薄膜处理技术，可用于调控等离子体材料的界面光学损耗和电子散射。然而，热退火对金薄膜/碳化硅基板界面在纳米尺度真空间隙中的近场辐射传热的具体影响尚未明确。本研究旨在填补这一空白。

Method: 研究人员通过实验测量了经过退火和未退火处理的金薄膜的介电函数，并在波动电动力学框架下评估了金/碳化硅界面的光谱和总辐射热通量。采用了模态分辨分析技术，揭示了表面等离子体模式的耦合变化。

Result: 研究发现，退火引起的金薄膜低频介电损耗的变化显著增强了界面处的倏逝电磁耦合，导致在几十纳米间距的近场辐射传热总量提升高达40%。这种增强源于过阻尼等离子体表面模式的耦合增强。

Conclusion: 研究表明，标准热退火技术为调控金属薄膜系统的界面辐射传热提供了一种实用方法，无需改变材料组成或几何结构。这为纳米尺度热调控和等离子体界面设计提供了重要指导。

Abstract: Thermal annealing is a widely used thin-film processing technique for modifying interfacial optical losses and electronic scattering in plasmonic materials. Here, we investigate how thermal annealing of gold thin films deposited on silicon carbide substrates influences interfacial near-field radiative heat transfer across nanoscale vacuum gaps. Using experimentally measured dielectric functions for annealed and unannealed Au films, we evaluate the spectral and total radiative heat flux between Au/SiC interfaces within a fluctuational electrodynamics framework.
  We show that annealing-induced changes in the low-frequency dielectric losses of Au significantly alter evanescent electromagnetic coupling at the interface, leading to enhancements of up to ~40\% in the total near-field radiative heat transfer at separations of tens of nanometers. Mode-resolved analysis reveals that this enhancement originates from strengthened coupling of overdamped plasmonic surface modes, which are highly sensitive to thin-film processing and interfacial microstructure. These results demonstrate that standard thermal annealing provides a practical route for tuning interfacial radiative heat transfer in metallic thin-film systems without modifying material composition or geometry, offering guidance for the design and interpretation of nanoscale thermal and plasmonic interfaces.
